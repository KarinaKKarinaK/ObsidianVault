## Practice Exam: Introduction to Psychology and its Methods (Hard Level)

This exam consists of 56 multiple-choice questions, with 4 questions dedicated to each of the 14 major topics covered in the course.

### Topic 1: Genes and Evolution

1. If two individuals possess identical **genotypes** yet exhibit measurable differences in a complex psychological **phenotype** (e.g., anxiety response), what must account for this variation? A. The inherent instability of the gene sequence. B. The influence of **polygenic inheritance** acting unpredictably. C. Differences in past and present **environmental** factors influencing gene expression. D. The unavoidable presence of spontaneous mutation between the two individuals.
    
2. For a property to be recessively inherited, it requires that: A. The individual must be homozygous for a dominant allele. B. The feature must be detrimental to the organism's survival. C. The **recessive allele** must be inherited from **both parents** for the property to be expressed in the phenotype. D. The expression is determined exclusively by the biochemical environment outside the cell.
    
3. In the context of **natural selection**, the specific trait that becomes better represented in the next generation is determined by its association with: A. The complexity of the genome inherited from ancestors. B. The degree to which the trait can be artificially engineered. C. The ability of the trait to confer a **higher survival and reproductive rate**. D. The sudden spontaneous generation of new neural pathways.
    
4. While external environment and developmental timing influence gene expression, the extent to which a gene is transcribed (turned 'on' or 'off') at any given moment is **most directly** regulated by: A. The overall inherited genotype of the organism. B. The presence of **specific chromosomes** (e.g., sex chromosomes). C. The **biochemical environment inside the cell**. D. The level of **heritability ratio (H)** observed in the population.
