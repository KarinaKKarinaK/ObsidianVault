# 1. Comparison of Major Theoretical Frameworks

|Dimension|Behaviorism|Cognitive Approach / SDT|Social [[Psychology]]|Media Equation|Agent-Based Modeling|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|Primary Focus|Observable behavior|Internal motives and representation|Social influence on thought and behavior|Human responses to media|Emergent macro-patterns from micro-rules|
|Level of Analysis|Individual organism|Individual psychological processes|Individual in social context|Individual interacting with media|Micro-to-macro system dynamics|
|View of Mind|Irrelevant or reducible|Central causal mechanism|Influenced by others’ presence|Automatically triggered social cognition|Often simplified or rule-based|
|Causation|External stimuli and reinforcement|Psychological mediators (motives, needs)|Social presence (actual, imagined, implied)|Automatic social scripts|Local interaction rules|
|Role of Environment|Determines behavior|Interpreted through representation|Shapes influence and norms|Provides social cues|Structures interaction|
|View of Internal Representation|Rejected or minimized|Essential|Implicitly assumed|Automatically activated|Often abstracted|
|Mechanism of Change|Reinforcement history|Need satisfaction, perceived autonomy|Norms, conformity, influence|Social rule activation|Iterated rule application|
|Emergence|Not emphasized|Not primary focus|Recognized but not formalized|Not primary focus|Central principle|
|Strengths|Experimental rigor, clarity|Explains motivation, meaning|Explains conformity and influence|Reveals automatic social cognition|Explains polarization and non-linearity|
|Weaknesses|Ignores meaning, qualia|Hard to formalize precisely|Often correlational|Limited to media context|Mapping to reality imperfect|

# 2. Behaviorism vs Cognitive vs Social vs ABM

|Question|Behaviorism|Cognitive / SDT|Social Psychology|ABM|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|What causes behavior?|Stimulus-response reinforcement|Motives, needs, internal representation|Presence of others|Local interaction rules|
|Are mental states real?|Not scientifically necessary|Yes, central|Yes|Simplified or modeled indirectly|
|Is meaning important?|No|Yes|Yes|Only if encoded in rules|
|Is behavior predictable?|Yes, through reinforcement schedules|Yes, via motivational structure|Yes, via social influence|Yes, via simulation|
|Can small differences matter?|Less emphasis|Yes|Yes|Strongly yes (non-linearity)|
|Does structure matter?|Environment only|Psychological and social structure|Social norms|Spatial and network structure|
|Does early randomness matter?|Less emphasized|Not central|Sometimes|Yes (path dependence)|

# 3. Exact vs Inexact Sciences

|Feature|Exact Sciences|Inexact Sciences|
|---|---|---|
|Measurement precision|High|Low to moderate|
|Mapping between theory and data|Direct|Proxy-based|
|Units standardized|Yes|Often abstract|
|Example domains|Physics|Social behavior|
|Role of formal models|Predictive precision|Clarification and insight|
|Risk|Overfitting precision|Conceptual vagueness|
|Importance of modeling|Mathematical necessity|Conceptual necessity|

---

# 4. Aggregate Models vs Agent-Based Models

|Feature|Aggregate Models|Agent-Based Models|
|---|---|---|
|Unit of analysis|Entire population|Individual agents|
|Heterogeneity|Ignored|Explicit|
|Local interaction|Implicit|Explicit|
|Emergence|Not modeled|Central|
|Non-linearity|Often smooth|Common|
|Feedback loops|Abstract|Mechanistic|
|Path dependence|Rare|Common|
|Example|Malthus growth|Homophily-opinion model|
|Best for|Large-scale trends|Complex social dynamics|

# 5. Opinion Dynamics Mechanisms

|Mechanism|Attraction|Repulsion|
|---|---|---|
|Trigger|Successful interaction|Failed interaction|
|Mathematical effect|Move toward other|Move away from other|
|Social interpretation|Influence|Rejection|
|Macro effect (low homophily)|Convergence|Mild clustering|
|Macro effect (high homophily)|Group convergence|Polarization|
|Stability|Promotes integration|Promotes extremization|
|Non-linearity|Moderate|Strong|

# 7. Types of Behaviorism

|Type|Core Claim|Focus|Limitation|
|---|---|---|---|
|Methodological|Study observable behavior only|Scientific method|Ignores internal states|
|Psychological|Reinforcement explains behavior|Learning histories|Cannot explain representation|
|Analytical|Mental states = behavioral dispositions|Philosophy of mind|Cannot explain qualia|

# 8. Reinforcement vs Representation

|Feature|Reinforcement Model|Representation Model|
|---|---|---|
|Behavior driven by|Past reward|Interpreted meaning|
|Example|Lever press → food|“This is ice cream”|
|Role of memory|Conditioning history|Cognitive interpretation|
|Flexibility|Limited|High|
|Supports adaptation to new context|Weak|Strong|

# 10. Micro-to-Macro Dynamics Across Theories

|Theory|Micro Mechanism|Macro Outcome|
|---|---|---|
|Behaviorism|Reinforcement|Habit formation|
|Cognitive|Representation|Meaning-driven action|
|Social Psychology|Social presence|Conformity|
|Media Equation|Social cue processing|Media personification|
|ABM (homophily)|Preference bias|Segregation|
|ABM (attraction)|Averaging|Consensus|
|ABM (repulsion)|Divergence|Polarization|

# 11. Strengths and Limits Across Frameworks

|Framework|What It Explains Well|What It Struggles With|
|---|---|---|
|Behaviorism|Conditioning|Internal meaning|
|Cognitive / SDT|Motivation and agency|Formal macro modeling|
|Social Psychology|Influence and conformity|Large-scale simulation|
|Media Equation|Human-media interaction|Structural power|
|ABM|Emergence and polarization|Psychological richness|

# 13. Homophily and System Outcomes

|Homophily Level|Interaction Pattern|Opinion Outcome|
|---|---|---|
|0 (heterophily)|Cross-group mixing|Convergence|
|0.5 (neutral)|Random mixing|Moderate convergence|
|1.0 (homophily)|In-group clustering|Persistent separation|

With repulsion:

- High homophily → strong polarization
    
- Low homophily → potential instability
